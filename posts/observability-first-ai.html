Observability-first AI

Published Oct 29, 2025 · 3 min read

If you can’t see it, you can’t trust it. AI systems need the same treatment as production software—metrics, logs, and traces that explain decisions and failures.

The minimal telemetry that matters

  • Request traces: inputs, retrieval hits, prompts, and outputs stitched together.
  • Key metrics: latency, cost per answer, retrieval precision, refusal rates.
  • Health signals: model drift indicators, spike detection, vendor outages.

Make debugging possible

  • Prompt/version control: every change is tracked with impact.
  • Deterministic evals: offline datasets for regression testing.
  • Incident runbooks: who does what when quality drops.

Rollout strategy

  1. Start with a golden-path use-case and instrument it deeply.
  2. Set thresholds (SLOs) for quality, cost, and latency.
  3. Only then scale to more users and use-cases.
