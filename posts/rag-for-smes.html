Why your SME doesn’t need an LLM—but does need a RAG pipeline

Published Oct 29, 2025 · 4 min read

Leaders ask for “an LLM.” What they actually want is trustworthy answers from their documents and systems. That’s a retrieval problem first, a generation problem second.

What reliable answers require

  • Authoritative sources: point to the doc/record, not just a sentence.
  • Up-to-date content: syncs from your SharePoint, Drive, tickets, DBs.
  • Auditability: show what was retrieved and why it was chosen.
  • Human-in-the-loop: allow confirmation/feedback where it matters.

A practical RAG blueprint

  1. Ingest: parse files (PDF, Office, HTML), normalize metadata, remove PII if required.
  2. Chunk: adaptive splitting by structure (headings, tables) to preserve context.
  3. Embed & index: store in a vector index; attach source IDs and permissions.
  4. Retrieve: hybrid search (semantic + keyword + filters) with reranking.
  5. Generate: compose answers with citations; refuse if confidence is low.
  6. Evaluate: offline eval sets + feedback loops; track precision/latency/cost.

Guardrails that matter for SMEs

  • Permissions: enforce access control at retrieval time.
  • Privacy: mask sensitive fields; log access to sources.
  • Observability: traces for retrieval + generation to debug failures.

When do you actually need an LLM?

Use a hosted or local LLM as a component for summarization and composing answers. The value comes from your data and the retrieval pipeline—not the model’s brand name.
